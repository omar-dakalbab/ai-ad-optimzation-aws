AMAZON ADS AI OPTIMIZER - HOW IT WORKS
=======================================

WHAT IS THIS?
-------------
This is an AI system that manages your Amazon Sponsored Products advertising
for you. Instead of you manually adjusting bids, budgets, and keywords every
day in Amazon Seller Central, this system does it automatically -- smarter and
faster than a human can.

Think of it like hiring a full-time PPC manager who works 24/7, never makes
emotional decisions, and learns from every dollar you spend.


WHAT DOES IT ACTUALLY DO?
-------------------------

1. CREATES YOUR CAMPAIGNS

   When you add a new product, the system automatically builds a proven
   3-campaign structure for it:

   - An AUTO campaign: Lets Amazon find relevant search terms for your
     product. This is your discovery engine -- it casts a wide net to find
     what shoppers are actually searching for.

   - A MANUAL EXACT campaign: Targets specific keywords with exact matching.
     These are your money-makers -- high intent, best return on ad spend.

   - A MANUAL BROAD campaign: Targets keywords with broad matching to
     discover new search terms you might be missing.

   The system figures out the starting bids from your product's profit margin.
   If your earbuds make $8 profit per sale, it calculates what you can afford
   to pay per click and still hit your target profitability. New campaigns
   start paused by default so you can review them before they go live.

   It also generates the initial keywords automatically from your product
   title. For example, "Wireless Bluetooth Earbuds with Charging Case" becomes
   keywords like "wireless bluetooth earbuds", "bluetooth earbuds",
   "wireless earbuds", "charging case", etc.


2. ADJUSTS YOUR BIDS EVERY DAY

   Every morning at 6 AM, the system looks at how every keyword performed and
   uses machine learning to predict what will happen next. Then it adjusts
   bids based on those predictions:

   - Keywords making you money? It increases the bid to get more traffic.
   - Keywords losing money? It lowers the bid to reduce waste.
   - Keywords getting lots of clicks but zero sales? It drops the bid to the
     minimum or pauses them entirely.

   The AI doesn't just look at yesterday. It analyzes trends over 7, 14, and
   30 days. A keyword that's been steadily improving gets treated differently
   than one that had one lucky day.

   SAFETY LIMITS:
   - Bids can only go up by 30% or down by 40% in a single day (no wild swings)
   - There's a floor of $0.10 and a ceiling of $15.00
   - Any bid over $8.00 gets flagged for your approval first
   - Everything runs in "dry run" mode by default -- it shows you what it
     WOULD do without actually doing it, until you turn on live mode


3. MOVES YOUR BUDGET TO WHERE IT WORKS BEST

   If you're running 10 campaigns, some will perform better than others. The
   system automatically shifts budget from underperforming campaigns to the
   ones with the best return.

   Example:
     Campaign A is getting $4 back for every $1 spent (great)
     Campaign B is getting $1.50 back for every $1 spent (not great)
     --> Move some of Campaign B's budget to Campaign A

   It moves a maximum of 15% of any campaign's budget per day, so changes
   are gradual and safe.


4. FINDS NEW KEYWORDS AND KILLS BAD ONES

   HARVESTING (finding winners):
   The auto and broad campaigns discover search terms that real shoppers use.
   When a search term gets at least 2 orders with a good conversion rate and
   profitable ACoS, the system automatically adds it as an exact-match keyword
   in your manual campaign. This is how your keyword list grows smarter over
   time.

   NEGATING (stopping waste):
   When a search term gets 15+ clicks and $20+ in spend with zero sales, the
   system adds it as a negative keyword so you stop paying for it. This is
   like plugging holes in a leaky bucket.

   PAUSING (cutting losses):
   Keywords that get 50+ clicks with zero orders, or consistently run at 2x
   your target ACoS over 30 days, get paused. They've had their chance.


HOW DOES THE AI LEARN?
----------------------
The system has machine learning models that predict three things for every
keyword, every day:

  1. Click-through rate: How likely shoppers are to click your ad
  2. Conversion rate: How likely clickers are to buy
  3. Revenue per sale: How much you'll make from a conversion

It uses 90 days of your actual performance data to train these models, and
retrains them every Sunday with the latest data. The predictions get better
over time as it accumulates more data about your specific products and market.

The AI considers dozens of factors:
  - How the keyword performed over the last 7, 14, and 30 days
  - Whether performance is trending up or down
  - Your product's price, profit margin, rating, and review count
  - Current inventory levels (no point advertising if you're about to run out)
  - Day of the week and seasonality (weekends behave differently)
  - Whether it's Prime Day or holiday season
  - How competitive the keyword appears to be


WHAT DATA DOES IT PULL FROM AMAZON?
------------------------------------
Every day, the system connects to two Amazon APIs:

  From Amazon Ads:
  - Your campaign structure (campaigns, ad groups, keywords)
  - Daily performance numbers (impressions, clicks, spend, orders, sales)
  - Search term reports (what people actually typed before clicking your ad)

  From Seller Central (SP-API):
  - Your product catalog (titles, categories, prices)
  - Order data (sales, revenue)
  - Inventory levels (FBA stock)
  - Sales and traffic reports

There's a 2-day lag on Amazon's advertising data (this is an Amazon limitation,
not ours), so the system always works with data from 2 days ago.


THE DAILY ROUTINE
-----------------
Here's what happens every day at 6 AM automatically:

  Step 1: Pull fresh data from Amazon
  Step 2: Create campaigns for any new products that don't have ads yet
  Step 3: Crunch the numbers (compute features for the ML models)
  Step 4: Run the AI predictions
  Step 5: (In parallel)
           - Calculate new bid recommendations
           - Calculate budget reallocation
           - Run keyword harvesting, negation, and pausing
  Step 6: Apply all the changes to your Amazon account

If anything fails, it retries twice with a 5-minute delay. If it still fails,
you get an email alert.


THE DASHBOARD
-------------
There's a visual dashboard where you can see everything that's happening:

  - Account Performance: Spend, sales, ACoS, and ROAS charted over time
  - Bid Changes: Every bid change the AI made, the old bid, new bid, and why
  - Model Accuracy: How well the AI's predictions matched reality
  - Budget Allocation: Where money is flowing between campaigns
  - Top Predictions: The AI's highest-confidence keyword opportunities


SAFETY AND CONTROL
------------------
The system is designed to be cautious:

  - DRY RUN MODE (on by default): Shows you what it would do without doing it.
    You have to explicitly turn this off when you're ready to go live.

  - APPROVAL GATE: Any bid above $8.00 needs your manual approval.

  - GRADUAL CHANGES: Maximum 30% bid increase or 40% decrease per day.
    Maximum 15% budget shift per day. No sudden dramatic moves.

  - FULL AUDIT TRAIL: Every single change is logged with the reason, the old
    value, the new value, and when it was applied. You can always see exactly
    what the AI did and why.

  - ROLLBACK: Because all previous bids are stored, you can reverse any
    change.

  - MINIMUM BUDGETS: Campaign budgets never drop below $5/day.
    Keyword bids never drop below $0.10.

  - SPEND CAP: Total daily spend can only increase by 20% maximum.


KEY NUMBERS YOU SHOULD KNOW
----------------------------
  Target ACoS:              25% (for every $1 in sales, you spend $0.25 on ads)
  Minimum acceptable ROAS:  3.0x (you get $3 back for every $1 spent)
  Default new product budget: $50/day (split across the 3 campaigns)
  Minimum bid:              $0.10
  Maximum bid:              $15.00
  Approval required above:  $8.00

All of these can be changed in the settings.


IN PLAIN ENGLISH
----------------
You list products on Amazon. This system watches how your ads perform, learns
what works and what doesn't, and automatically adjusts everything to make you
more money while spending less on ads. It creates the campaigns, picks the
keywords, sets the bids, moves the budgets, kills the waste, and scales the
winners -- every single day, without you having to touch Amazon's ad console.
